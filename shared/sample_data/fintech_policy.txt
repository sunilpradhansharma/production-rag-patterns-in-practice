MERIDIAN BANK — CONSUMER LENDING POLICY
Version 4.2 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Classification: Internal Use Only

================================================================================
SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

1.1 Purpose
This policy establishes the standards and procedures for the origination,
underwriting, and management of consumer lending products at Meridian Bank
("the Bank"). It applies to all personal loans, auto loans, home equity lines
of credit (HELOCs), and unsecured revolving credit products.

1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
  a) All Bank employees involved in the lending process
  b) Third-party originators operating under a Meridian Bank agreement
  c) Automated decision systems processing consumer loan applications

1.3 Regulatory Framework
The Bank's consumer lending activities are governed by:
  - Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) and Regulation B
  - Fair Housing Act (FHA)
  - Truth in Lending Act (TILA) and Regulation Z
  - Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
  - Community Reinvestment Act (CRA)

SECTION 2: CREDIT ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
================================================================================

2.1 Minimum Eligibility Requirements
All applicants must meet the following minimum requirements at the time of
application:

  | Criterion              | Minimum Standard         | Notes                        |
  |------------------------|--------------------------|------------------------------|
  | Credit Score (FICO 8)  | 620                      | Applies to primary applicant |
  | Debt-to-Income (DTI)   | ≤ 43%                    | See Section 2.3 for calc     |
  | Employment Status      | Employed or self-employed| Min. 2 years history         |
  | Age                    | 18 years or older        | Legal minimum                |
  | Residency              | US resident              | Valid SSN required           |

2.2 Credit Score Tiers
The Bank uses a tiered pricing model based on the primary applicant's FICO 8
score at the time of application:

  | Tier | FICO Range | Risk Category | Rate Adjustment |
  |------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
  | A+   | 780–850   | Exceptional   | –1.50%          |
  | A    | 740–779   | Very Good     | –0.75%          |
  | B    | 700–739   | Good          | Base Rate       |
  | C    | 660–699   | Fair          | +1.25%          |
  | D    | 620–659   | Poor          | +2.50%          |
  | NQ   | < 620     | Non-Qualifying| Not eligible    |

2.3 Debt-to-Income Calculation
DTI is calculated as monthly debt obligations divided by gross monthly income.
Monthly debt obligations include:
  - Monthly payment on the proposed loan
  - Minimum payments on all revolving credit accounts
  - Scheduled payments on all installment loans
  - Monthly rent or mortgage payment (for rental properties, net rental income
    may offset up to 75% of the obligation)

2.4 Income Documentation Requirements
  a) W-2 employees: Two most recent pay stubs AND most recent W-2
  b) Self-employed: Two years of signed federal tax returns AND current
     year profit/loss statement prepared by a CPA
  c) Social Security/pension: Award letter dated within 12 months
  d) Investment income: Two years of 1099 statements

SECTION 3: PRODUCT PARAMETERS
================================================================================

3.1 Personal Loan
  - Loan amounts: $2,500 – $50,000
  - Term: 24 – 84 months
  - Minimum FICO: 660 (Tier C)
  - Maximum DTI: 40%
  - No collateral required
  - Origination fee: 1.0% – 6.0% (deducted from proceeds)
  - Prepayment penalty: None

3.2 Auto Loan (New Vehicle)
  - Loan amounts: $5,000 – $100,000
  - Term: 24 – 84 months
  - Minimum FICO: 620 (Tier D, but rate adjusted)
  - Maximum LTV: 110% (includes taxes, fees, and GAP insurance)
  - Maximum DTI: 43%
  - Vehicle age: New vehicles only (model year current or previous)

3.3 Auto Loan (Used Vehicle)
  - Loan amounts: $5,000 – $75,000
  - Term: 24 – 72 months
  - Minimum FICO: 640
  - Maximum LTV: 100%
  - Vehicle age: 7 years or newer; mileage ≤ 100,000

3.4 Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)
  - Credit line amounts: $25,000 – $500,000
  - Draw period: 10 years
  - Repayment period: 20 years
  - Minimum FICO: 680
  - Maximum CLTV: 85%
  - Maximum DTI: 43%
  - Appraisal required for lines ≥ $250,000

SECTION 4: UNDERWRITING PROCESS
================================================================================

4.1 Automated Underwriting
The Bank's proprietary Automated Underwriting System (AUS) evaluates all
incoming applications against the criteria in Section 2. Applications
receiving an AUS "Approve" decision and meeting all product parameters in
Section 3 may be funded without manual review.

4.2 Manual Review Triggers
The following conditions require escalation to a human underwriter:
  a) AUS decision of "Refer" or "Refer with Caution"
  b) FICO score < 660 for personal loans
  c) DTI between 40% and 43% for any product
  d) Self-employment income > 50% of qualifying income
  e) Loan amount > $75,000 for unsecured products
  f) Any derogatory credit event within 24 months of application
     (foreclosure, bankruptcy discharge, repossession, charge-off)

4.3 Adverse Action Procedures
When an application is denied, the Bank must:
  1. Provide written notice within 30 days of the credit decision
  2. State the specific reasons for the adverse action (minimum 4 reasons
     from the ECOA Appendix C list)
  3. Provide the applicant's credit score and the range of scores used
  4. Provide contact information for all credit bureaus queried

SECTION 5: FAIR LENDING AND COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

5.1 Prohibited Bases for Credit Decisions
Lending decisions may not be based on:
  - Race, color, religion, national origin, sex
  - Marital status or familial status
  - Age (provided the applicant is of legal age to contract)
  - Receipt of income from public assistance programs
  - Exercise of rights under the Consumer Credit Protection Act

5.2 Annual Fair Lending Review
The Compliance Department conducts an annual statistical analysis of:
  a) Application approval rates by protected class
  b) Pricing disparities by protected class
  c) Geographic distribution of approvals (CRA compliance)

Results are reported to the Board Risk Committee no later than March 31
of each calendar year.

5.3 CRA Assessment Area
Meridian Bank's primary CRA assessment area includes all MSAs in which the
Bank maintains a physical branch presence. Lending activity outside the
assessment area is tracked separately but does not count toward CRA credit.

SECTION 6: EXCEPTIONS AND OVERRIDES
================================================================================

6.1 Policy Exception Authority
Exceptions to the minimum eligibility criteria may be granted as follows:

  | Exception Type         | Approval Authority         | Max Frequency        |
  |------------------------|----------------------------|----------------------|
  | FICO 580–619           | Regional Credit Officer    | 5% of monthly volume |
  | DTI 43%–50%            | Chief Credit Officer       | 2% of monthly volume |
  | LTV > 110% (auto)      | Regional Credit Officer    | Not permitted        |
  | Income documentation   | Branch Manager + Underwriter| Case by case        |

6.2 Compensating Factors
When granting exceptions, underwriters must document at least two of the
following compensating factors:
  - Liquid reserves ≥ 6 months of loan payments
  - Stable employment at same employer ≥ 5 years
  - Low utilization ratio (< 20%) on revolving accounts
  - Strong payment history (no 30-day late payments in 36 months)
  - Co-applicant with FICO ≥ 740

================================================================================
SECTION 7: DOCUMENT RETENTION
================================================================================

7.1 Retention Schedule
  - Approved loan files: 7 years from origination date
  - Denied applications: 25 months from date of adverse action notice
  - AUS findings: 3 years from date of credit decision
  - Fair lending analysis: Permanently retained

7.2 Electronic Records
All loan documentation may be retained in electronic form provided:
  a) Records are stored in an approved Document Management System (DMS)
  b) Records are protected against alteration after loan closing
  c) Records can be reproduced in legible paper form within 3 business days
