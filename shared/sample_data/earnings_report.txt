EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

Pinnacle Financial Group ("Pinnacle" or "the Company") reported net income of
$1.43 billion for the third quarter of 2024, or $2.87 per diluted share, compared
with $1.31 billion, or $2.61 per diluted share, for Q3 2023. Return on average
equity was 13.8%, and return on average assets was 1.24%. Pre-provision net
revenue (PPNR) rose 7.2% year-over-year to $2.09 billion, reflecting continued
growth in net interest income and controlled expense growth.

"Our Q3 results demonstrate the strength of our diversified business model,"
said Chairman and CEO Robert Ashford. "Retail lending benefited from mortgage
refinance activity, commercial banking delivered record middle-market originations,
and our investment banking division closed three landmark M&A advisory mandates.
We are raising full-year 2024 EPS guidance to reflect this momentum."

RETAIL BANKING
================================================================================

Retail Banking encompasses consumer deposit-taking, personal lending, mortgage
origination, auto loans, home equity products, and small business banking for
companies with annual revenue below $10 million.

Net revenue of $1.84 billion in Q3 2024 grew 7.8% versus Q3 2023, driven by:

  Mortgage Banking: Mortgage origination volume reached $8.2 billion, up 31%
  year-over-year, as refinance activity accelerated following 50-basis-point
  Federal Reserve rate cuts in Q3 2024. Purchase originations were flat at
  $4.7 billion; refi volume surged to $3.5 billion from $1.1 billion in Q3 2023.
  Gain-on-sale margin compressed to 2.74% (Q3 2023: 3.18%) as competition
  intensified. Mortgage servicing rights (MSR) portfolio declined in fair value
  by $34 million due to the rate environment.

  Consumer Lending: Personal loan originations of $1.4 billion were up 9% YoY.
  Average personal loan balances of $6.2 billion carried a net yield of 9.47%.
  Net charge-off rate of 0.42% remained below the internal target of 0.50%.
  Delinquency (30+ DPD) was 1.18%, flat with the prior quarter.

  Deposits: Average retail deposits of $89.4 billion declined $1.2 billion
  sequentially as higher-rate CD maturities were partially offset by growth in
  money market accounts. Retail deposit beta was 42% for the quarter, consistent
  with guidance. Non-interest-bearing deposits stabilized at 24% of the retail
  deposit mix.

COMMERCIAL BANKING
================================================================================

Commercial Banking serves companies with annual revenue between $10 million and
$2 billion, providing credit, treasury management, capital markets access,
and international banking services.

Net revenue of $1.25 billion in Q3 2024 increased 8.3% year-over-year.

  Middle-Market Lending: Average commercial loan balances grew to $61.7 billion,
  up 5.1% annualized from Q2 2024. New commitment volume of $9.3 billion was the
  highest quarterly total in the Company's history, led by manufacturing, healthcare,
  and technology verticals. Weighted average new origination yield was 7.62%,
  up 12 bps from Q2 2024 as floating-rate floors were exceeded on more credits.

  Credit Quality: Net charge-offs in Commercial Banking were $27.8 million (0.18%
  annualized NCO rate), compared to $24.1 million in Q3 2023. Non-accrual loans
  increased to $412 million (0.67% of commercial loans), driven by two office
  real estate credits totalling $118 million that were moved to non-accrual status
  following valuation declines in Central Business District markets. The allowance
  for credit losses (ACL) on commercial loans was $1.04 billion, representing
  an ACL/loan ratio of 1.69%.

  Treasury Management: Fee revenue from treasury management services grew 11.4%
  to $187 million, supported by expanded adoption of the Company's integrated
  payables and receivables automation platform. Active treasury management
  clients reached 14,200, up 8% from Q3 2023.

INVESTMENT BANKING
================================================================================

Investment Banking provides M&A advisory, equity underwriting, debt capital
markets, structured finance, and institutional sales and trading for corporate,
financial sponsor, and government clients.

Net revenue of $614 million in Q3 2024 increased 16.1% year-over-year, the
strongest quarterly result in three years.

  Advisory: M&A advisory fees of $228 million reflected the closing of three
  landmark transactions, including a $4.1 billion industrial sector merger and
  two financial sponsor-led buyouts. The pipeline of announced but unclosed
  transactions stands at $18.7 billion as of September 30, 2024.

  Equity Capital Markets: Underwriting fees of $134 million were up 42% YoY,
  reflecting a recovery in IPO volumes (7 deals, $3.2 billion aggregate proceeds)
  and elevated follow-on activity ($5.8 billion in 14 follow-on offerings).

  Debt Capital Markets: DCM fees of $106 million were flat year-over-year.
  Investment-grade issuance activity of $22.4 billion was offset by a slowdown
  in leveraged finance. High-yield volumes declined 18% to $3.1 billion as
  sponsors waited for further rate clarity before funding new LBOs.

  Sales & Trading: Markets revenue of $146 million included Fixed Income of
  $98 million and Equities of $48 million. Fixed income benefited from elevated
  rates volatility in Q3 2024; equity markets revenue was down 12% YoY on lower
  cash equity volumes.
