EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

Apex Payments Inc. ("Apex" or "the Company") reported strong third-quarter
results driven by growth in its Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) segment and continued
expansion of its B2B payment processing vertical. Total revenue reached $847.3
million for Q3 2025, representing a 23.4% year-over-year increase. Net income
attributable to common stockholders was $112.8 million, or $0.89 per diluted
share, compared to $74.1 million, or $0.59 per diluted share, in Q3 2024.

"We delivered another record quarter," said CEO Miranda Chen. "Our merchant
network now exceeds 2.1 million active partners globally, and BNPL originations
crossed $3 billion for the first time in a single quarter. We remain on track
to achieve our full-year revenue guidance of $3.2–3.3 billion."

MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Business Segment Performance

PAYMENT PROCESSING
Payment processing revenue grew 20.6% year-over-year to $412.1 million.
Total Payment Volume (TPV) reached $89.4 billion for the quarter, up 24.1%
from $72.0 billion in Q3 2024. Take rate declined modestly from 0.474% to
0.461% due to mix shift toward enterprise merchants who receive volume-based
pricing. The Company added 127,000 net new merchant accounts during the quarter,
bringing the total active merchant base to 2.1 million globally.

International markets now represent 31% of payment processing revenue, up from
24% in the prior year period, driven primarily by expansion in Southeast Asia
and continued growth in the UK and Germany.

BUY NOW, PAY LATER
BNPL originations of $3.04 billion for Q3 2025 exceeded the $3 billion milestone
for the first time in a single quarter, representing 47.2% growth versus
$2.07 billion in Q3 2024. Active BNPL users reached 18.7 million, up 38.1%
year-over-year.

Credit performance has remained within our underwriting targets. The 30-day+
delinquency rate on the managed BNPL portfolio was 2.84% at September 30, 2025,
compared to 2.91% at June 30, 2025 and 2.67% at September 30, 2024. Net charge-off
rate for the trailing twelve months was 4.12%, consistent with our full-year
guidance of 4.0%–4.5%.

The Company completed a $1.5 billion BNPL whole loan sale to institutional
investors during the quarter, generating a gain on sale of $18.4 million and
recycling capital for new originations.

SUBSCRIPTIONS AND SAAS
Subscription revenue grew 22.6% year-over-year to $143.7 million. Annual
Recurring Revenue (ARR) reached $596 million at quarter end, up from $476 million
one year prior. Net Revenue Retention (NRR) was 118%, indicating strong expansion
within existing accounts. The Company launched its new AI-powered fraud detection
module in August 2025, which has been adopted by 847 enterprise clients to date.

Liquidity and Capital Resources
At September 30, 2025, the Company had $1.84 billion in unrestricted cash and
$750 million in undrawn capacity on its revolving credit facility, providing
total liquidity of approximately $2.59 billion. The Company repurchased 1.4 million
shares for $98.2 million during the quarter at an average price of $70.14 per share.
$401.8 million remains available under the current repurchase authorization.
